# Basic Computer Engineering — Grading Methodology

## 1. What earns each grade

The grade is built from the **five course outcomes** (CLO1–CLO5). For each one a student reaches **Mastery** (does exactly what the outcome asks — the target) or **Exemplary** (goes beyond it, on the Final Project, or Lab 7 for CLO4).

**Meet the target on all five outcomes and the student earns a B. Exemplary work is what lifts the grade to B+ and A.**

| Profile across the five outcomes | Score | % | Grade |
|---|:--:|:--:|:--:|
| 5 Exemplary | 4.00 | 100% | A |
| 3 Mastery + 2 Exemplary | 3.40 | 85% | A |
| **4 Mastery + 1 Exemplary** | 3.20 | 80% | **B+** |
| **5 Mastery** | 3.00 | 75% | **B** |
| 4 Mastery + 1 Near Mastery | 2.90 | 72.5% | B |
| 3 Mastery + 2 Near Mastery | 2.80 | 70% | C+ |
| 3 Mastery + 2 Below Mastery | 2.60 | 65% | C |
| 5 Near Mastery | 2.50 | 62.5% | C |
| 5 Below Mastery | 2.00 | 50% | F |

**Meet every target: B. One Exemplary: B+. Two or more Exemplary: A.**

## 2. How the grade is calculated

### From rubric levels to a CLO score

Each criterion is scored 0–4:

| Rubric level | Score |
|---|---|
| Exemplary | 4 |
| Mastery | 3 |
| Near Mastery | 2.5 |
| Below Mastery | 2 |
| No Evidence | 0 |

A **CLO score** is the equal-weight average of its criterion scores.

> Level 0 is reserved for nothing submitted. Submitted work that falls short still earns at least 2 (Below Mastery).

### From CLO scores to a final grade

Your **final score** (out of 4) is the equal-weight average of your five CLO scores:

```
Final score  =  (sum of the 5 CLO scores) ÷ 5
```

This score is then converted to a course percentage:

```
Course %  =  final score ÷ 4 × 100
```

Mapped to a letter grade:

| Score (out of 4) | Course % | Grade |
|:--:|:--:|:--:|
| 3.24 – 4.00 | 81–100 | **A**  |
| 3.04 – 3.23 | 76–80  | **B+** |
| 2.84 – 3.03 | 71–75  | **B**  |
| 2.64 – 2.83 | 66–70  | **C+** |
| 2.44 – 2.63 | 61–65  | **C**  |
| 2.24 – 2.43 | 56–60  | **D+** |
| 2.04 – 2.23 | 51–55  | **D**  |
| 0.00 – 2.03 | 0–50   | **F**  |

> Example: a final score of 3.24 (= 81%) earns an A, while 3.21 (≈ 80%) earns a B+.

> An **A** starts at a score of **3.24 (81%)**, not 3.20 (80%): it takes more than four Mastery + one Exemplary CLOs, since that profile lands at exactly 3.20 (80%), a B+.
