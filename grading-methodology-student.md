# Basic Computer Engineering — How Your Grade Works

Your grade is built from the **five course outcomes** (CLO1–CLO5). For each one you aim to reach:

- **Mastery** — you did exactly what the outcome asks. **This is the target.**
- **Exemplary** — you went beyond it (on the **Final Project**, or **Lab 7** for CLO4).

**Meet the target on all five outcomes and you earn a B. Exemplary work is what lifts you to B+ and A.**

## What earns each grade

| Across the five outcomes | Grade |
|---|:--:|
| Exemplary on all 5 | **A** |
| Mastery on 3, Exemplary on 2 | **A** |
| Mastery on 4, Exemplary on 1 | **B+** |
| **Mastery on all 5** | **B** |
| Mastery on 3, the rest Near Mastery | C+ |
| Mastery on 3, the rest Below Mastery | C |

So aim for Mastery everywhere first — then turn two outcomes into Exemplary to reach an A.

---

## How the grade is calculated

Each rubric level is worth points:

| Level | Points |
|---|:--:|
| Exemplary | 4 |
| Mastery | 3 |
| Near Mastery | 2.5 |
| Below Mastery | 2 |
| No Evidence | 0 |

> Level 0 is only for **nothing submitted**. Submitted work that falls short still earns at least **2**.

- Each **CLO score** is the average of its criteria.
- Your **final score** (out of 4) is the average of your five CLO scores.

| Final score | Grade |
|:--:|:--:|
| 3.24 – 4.00 | **A**  |
| 3.04 – 3.23 | **B+** |
| 2.84 – 3.03 | **B**  |
| 2.64 – 2.83 | **C+** |
| 2.44 – 2.63 | **C**  |
| 2.24 – 2.43 | **D+** |
| 2.04 – 2.23 | **D**  |
| 0.00 – 2.03 | **F**  |
